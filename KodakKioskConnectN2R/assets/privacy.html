Personal Privacy Statement for Kodak Kiosk Wireless Connect App

The Eastman Kodak Company (“Kodak”) is committed to protecting your privacy online. Our pledge is to safeguard any personal information that you provide us, and to make every reasonable effort to use this information only as you choose.  Kodak is also a participant in the Safe Harbor program between the US and the European Union, as well as the Safe Harbor program between the U.S. and Switzerland.  Any personal data collected as described below will be transmitted to Kodak’s servers in the United States or Europe and handled as required by the Safe Harbor principles.

Scope of This Notice

The goal of this Privacy Notice is to advise you of the personal information that Kodak collects when you use the Kiosk Wireless Connect App (“the App”), and how we use that information.  The Privacy Notice also describes the choices you have regarding how this information is used.

How We Collect and Use Your Personal Information

Kodak's primary goals in collecting your personal information are to:

·       provide an enjoyable, personalized experience while using the App,

·       tailor our communications to match your interests, and

·       facilitate the development of new products and services relevant to consumers like you.

 

Here are the types of personal information we gather, and how we use it.

  1. Information Shared When You Create or Order a Photo Product On-line
  1. When you are in the process of working on a photo product in the prints workflow, the App is using a Kodak server. A Kodak session id is stored locally on your mobile device so that you can continue working on photo products using the application. This information is deleted if you start the application again.  Session information is deleted after the order has been placed.
  2. When you are in the process of working on a photo product, the following information regarding each selected photo is also sent to Kodak: the photo image and its associated image tags that are contained in the image file. This information is temporarily stored at Kodak and then deleted after 2 days with no continued activity, or when you begin using the App again. When you print a photo product, this information is temporarily stored at Kodak and then deleted after 30 days.
  3. When you submit for printing a photo product, the App may ask you for identification information. This data may include your first name, last name, telephone number, and e-mail address.  This data will be associated with your photo product order.
  1. Information We Automatically Collect
  1. At installation time, you will be asked for your consent to track your usage of the App and to record non-personally identifiable data.  This data will be recorded in the form of Cookies and web beacons.
  2. Cookies:  Cookies are small pieces of information stored by the App in your mobile device, at a web site's request. Kodak cookies do not contain any personal information, but are used primarily as follows:
  1. to help us understand the size of our audience and traffic patterns;
  2. to collect and record information about how the aggregate group of users employed the App to create photo products;
  3. to manage and present site information and the pictures displayed on your computer, and
  4. to deliver information specific to your interests.
  1. Web Beacons: We also place small "tracker gifs" or "beacons" on many of the screens in our App. We use these beacons, in connection with cookies, to collect non-personal data on the usage of our App including but not limited to the date and time of your usage, the screens or functions used, the type of operating system (e.g., iOS or Android), and the domain name of the user's Internet service provider (e.g., Verizon). This information is collected about thousands of App uses and analyzed as a whole. This information is useful in, for example, tracking the performance of our App.
  2. IP Address:   For on-line orders, Kodak analyzes IP addresses in the aggregate for system administration purposes and to gather broad demographic information. We do not link IP addresses to your mobile device’s ID.

Does Kodak Share Your Information?

Your personally identifiable information will not be rented or sold to any third party. Kodak may share your personal information as follows:

·       Companies who work on our behalf: We contract with other companies to perform business support functions on our behalf, which may involve limited access to your personal information. We require these companies to use the information only to provide the contracted services; they are prohibited from transferring the information to another party except as needed to provide those services. Some examples of these business support functions include order fulfillment, coupon fulfillment, award and rebate fulfillments, customer survey execution; and information systems management. We also may make aggregate non-personal information available to select service providers.

·       Subsidiaries, parent corporations and joint ventures:  A subsidiary or a joint venture is an organization in which Eastman Kodak Company ("EKC") owns at least a 50% interest. EKC is the "parent corporation" to each of its subsidiaries. We may share your information with an EKC subsidiary or joint venture partner, with the requirement that they not transfer your information to another party for marketing purposes, or use your information contrary to your expressed choices. Similarly, if an EKC subsidiary or joint venture shares your information with us, we will not transfer your information to another party for marketing purposes or use your information contrary to your expressed choices.

·       Business Transfers: Kodak reserves the right to transfer your personal information in connection with the sale or transfer of all or a portion of our business or assets. If the business is sold or transferred, Kodak will give you an opportunity to tell us not to transfer your personal information. In some cases, this may mean that the new organization will not be able to continue providing to you the services or products that Kodak provided.

·       Law Enforcement: Kodak may disclose personal information to third parties without your consent for any of the following reasons: to comply with any law, regulation or court order, to cooperate with Government investigations, to help prevent fraud or to enforce or protect the rights of Kodak or its subsidiaries.

·       With Your Consent: In all other cases, Kodak will obtain your express consent before sharing your information with third parties. For example, Kodak may conduct a joint offer or contest with a third party and you will be asked whether your personal information should be shared with that third party.

Children's Privacy

Protecting the online privacy of children is especially important, and those under the age of 13 are protected by United States federal law. For that reason, Kodak does not knowingly permit children under the age of 13 to download the App, or to buy goods and services on our sites, without verifiable parental consent. Kodak does not knowingly collect or solicit personal information about children under 13, except with their parent's express consent.

If we ever include children under the age of 13 as part of our intended site audience, those specific web pages will, in accordance with the provisions of the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), be clearly identified and provide an explicit privacy notice; and we will provide processes to obtain parental approval, provide access to information and allow parents to request removal of their children's personal information.

Kodak encourages parents and guardians to spend time with their children online and to participate in their interactive activities and interests.
